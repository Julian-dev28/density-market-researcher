## 4. CROSS-MARKET SYNTHESIS

### The Macro-Crypto Nexus

Three critical interactions between macro and crypto right now:

**1. Fed Pause = Crypto Purgatory, But Not Death**
The Fed at 3.64% with sticky core inflation means no near-term rate cuts. This removes the primary catalyst for a crypto bull market (liquidity expansion). However, the easing cycle is not over — it's paused. The PPI deflation (-0.26%) is a leading indicator that core CPI will moderate in 2-3 months, reopening the door for cuts. **Crypto is pricing in the pause; it has not priced in the eventual resumption of cuts.**

**2. Stablecoin Growth Mirrors Monetary Conditions**
The +54% growth in stablecoin supply to $285B is partially a function of the broader macro environment: high nominal rates make holding USD-pegged stablecoins attractive (many yield 4-5% on-chain). As rates decline further, the opportunity cost of deploying into risk assets falls, creating a **natural catalyst for rotation out of stables into BTC/alts.**

**3. Consumer Sentiment Links Both Markets**
UMich sentiment at 56.4 (22nd percentile) and crypto Fear & Greed at 8 tell the same story: **broad-based risk aversion.** But both are mean-reverting indicators at extremes. The equity market has rallied despite depressed sentiment (SPY implied ~+10% YTD given sector returns); crypto has not. This divergence suggests crypto is pricing in more pessimism than warranted by the actual economic data.

**4. Housing Recovery Is Bullish for Crypto-Correlated Equities**
The housing recovery (starts +6.2%, mortgage rates at 52w lows) reflects a rate-sensitive economy that responds to easing. This same rate-sensitivity applies to crypto and growth assets. If housing is turning, crypto-adjacent equities (COIN, MSTR, MARA) are likely mispriced relative to the macro trajectory.
